About Me

I am a PhD student in Applied Mathematics at Politecnico di Torino, working under the supervision of Prof. Francesco Vaccarino and Prof. Giovanni Petri. My research interests lie at the intersection of mathematics, computation, and cognition, with a particular focus on understanding complex systems through topological and network-theoretic lenses.

My current work explores how topological methods can provide insights into neural network architectures and cognitive processes, particularly examining capacity constraints that appear in both biological and artificial intelligence systems.

Research Interests

Applied Topology

Investigating topological data analysis methods and their applications to complex data structures.

Network Science

Studying the structure and dynamics of complex networks across various domains.

Neural Networks

Exploring theoretical and topological aspects of neural network architectures and their learning dynamics.

Cognitive Psychology

Examining capacity constraints in human cognition and artificial systems from a mathematical perspective.
