About

I am a PhD student in Applied Mathematics at Politecnico di Torino, supervised by Francesco Vaccarino and Giovanni Petri. My work sits at the intersection of topology, network science, and learning theory.

I am particularly interested in how structural and topological constraints shape representation, capacity, and generalization in both biological and artificial systems.

Research Interests

Applied & Algebraic Topology

Topological data analysis, homology, and discrete calculus for structured data.

Network Science

Geometry and dynamics of complex networks and higher-order interactions.

Learning Theory

Theoretical properties of neural networks, capacity, and inductive bias.

Cognition

Mathematical perspectives on cognitive constraints and representations.
